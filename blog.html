Project Summary

This site is a personal system for living by values while training the nervous system through mindfulness, slow living, and deliberate awareness.

The goal of the site is to document and share a structured approach to nervous system healing and mindful productivity.

The system is built around the PCNN method (Pause, Curious, Name, Notice), slow deliberate actions such as mindful typing and movement.

Core Ideas

  • Live according to clear personal values.
  • Slow down actions and thinking.
  • Train the nervous system through daily practices.
  • Observe thoughts and body sensations without reacting immediately.
  • Use simple routines to reset the mind and body.

ACT Inspired Mind Training

What This Practice Trains

This section is inspired by Acceptance and Commitment Therapy. The goal is not to eliminate thoughts or emotions. The goal is to develop psychological flexibility — the ability to stay present, observe the mind clearly, and take actions aligned with your values.

1. Present Moment Awareness

Pause and notice what is happening right now. Notice your breath. Notice your posture. Notice the sensations in your body.

Nothing needs to be changed. Simply observe.

2. Cognitive Defusion

When a thought appears, step back slightly and say:

“I am noticing the thought that…”

Example:
“I am noticing the thought that I might fail.”

This helps create space between you and the thought. Thoughts become events in the mind, not commands.

3. Acceptance

Instead of fighting difficult emotions, allow them to exist for a moment.

You might say:

“It is okay for this feeling to be here.”

Emotions often soften when they are allowed rather than resisted.

4. The Observing Self

Notice that there is a part of you that can observe thoughts, feelings, and sensations.

Thoughts change. Emotions change. Experiences change.

But the observing awareness remains steady.

5. Values

Ask yourself:

“What kind of person do I want to be?”

Values are directions rather than goals. Examples include:

  • Curiosity
  • Kindness
  • Growth
  • Integrity
  • Patience

6. Committed Action

Once values are clear, take small actions aligned with them.

Even when thoughts or emotions are uncomfortable, you can still choose actions that reflect who you want to be.

Small consistent actions gradually shape your life.
