Mindful Daily Awareness Practice - part 1

  • Waking up slowly and mindfully. Brush teeth slowly and drink coffee with full awareness.
  • Check emails and text messages mindfully. Breathe for 5 seconds and do everything at a slow, calm pace.
  • Talk slowly and deliberately. Listen carefully to sounds. Notice smell, taste, vision, and the environment.
  • During meetings, chats, and emails observe feelings and body sensations. Notice others in the meeting. Take pauses for breath and awareness.

Mindful Daily Awareness Practice - part 2

  • While walking, stop every 5 steps and take a breath. Observe from the outside: how you are sitting, where you are looking, and how fast your hands are typing.
  • When writing or reading, move slowly. Observe the monitor. Breathe, feel the body, stretch gently, and scan for tension.

Mindful Daily Awareness Practice - part 3

  • Before bed, fix the bed slowly. Take 5–10 second breaths. While cooking, use breathing instead of a timer (for example: tortilla 10 seconds, flip for 5 seconds).
  • Type each word slowly and take a breath between thoughts.

Values & Coping Skills

A quick-reference area for daily alignment, calm, and renewal.

Core Values

Healing & Growth

I commit to ongoing inner healing, learning from pain rather than avoiding it. Growth means patience, self-compassion, and trusting that small steps matter.
  • Gentle self-care over self-criticism
  • Learning from setbacks instead of judging them
  • Choosing progress, not perfection

Awareness & Reflection

I slow down enough to notice my thoughts, emotions, and body signals. Awareness gives me choice instead of reaction.
  • Pause and name what I’m feeling
  • Curiosity instead of fear
  • Reflect before responding

Discipline & Practice

I build stability through small, repeatable habits. Discipline supports my freedom and well-being.
  • Daily routines that calm my nervous system
  • Consistency over intensity
  • Showing up even when motivation is low

Respect & Boundaries

I honor my limits and the limits of others. Boundaries protect my energy, safety, and peace.
  • Saying no without guilt
  • Not absorbing others’ emotions
  • Choosing distance when needed

Spiritual Connection

I nurture a quiet connection to meaning, presence, and gratitude. Stillness is a source of grounding and renewal.
  • Breath, silence, and gentle awareness
  • Gratitude for small moments
  • Trust in something larger than myself

Authenticity

I choose to be real with myself and others. Honesty creates clarity, safety, and self-respect.
  • Speaking simply and truthfully
  • Letting go of people-pleasing
  • Living in alignment with my values

Coping Skills

Quiet Focus Block

Reduce input to settle the nervous system.

  • Noise reduction: headset/ear muffs; silent room (closet).
  • Visual nature loop on a monitor (no sound).
  • Cartoon/animation visuals with sound off.
  • Avoid music + chemicals combo (known trigger).
CalmFocus

Body Reset

Gentle movement to discharge and rebalance.

  • Qigong: 6-Dimensional Self-Healing Gongs (10–30 min).
  • 7-Minute Chi (evenings).
  • Tai Chi forms; slow breathing with movement.
  • "Shaking"/loosening for 1–3 minutes.
  • Stationary bike or light weights (Planet Fitness).
BodyCalm

Breath & Grounding

Downshift quickly and re-orient to the present.

  • 4-6 breathing (inhale 4, exhale 6) × 8–12 reps.
  • Feet + seat check (notice contact points; soften jaw/shoulders).
  • Label: “I notice ___ and I’m safe right now.”

Dantian Visualization (Blue Sky / Golden Light)

Quiet imagery to center attention in the lower abdomen.

  • Rest hands over lower dantian; breathe softly into the belly.
  • Imagine a clear blue sky or warm golden light filling the lower dantian for 3–5 minutes.
  • Finish with two slower exhalations and a gentle posture check.

Journaling & Reflection

Simple prompts to capture learning and reset.

Creative & Play

Light, low-stakes activities that restore.

Environment & Sensory

Adjust surroundings to reduce overwhelm.

  • Temperature comfort: avoid too hot/humid/cold; adjust layers/fan.
  • Outdoor insect/ambient noise plan (crickets): move indoors or mask.
  • Lighting: softer lamp vs. overhead glare.

Social & Boundaries

Keep interactions aligned with energy levels.

  • Choose shorter check-ins over long calls when taxed.
  • Use plain, respectful language; skip sarcasm when stressed.
  • “I’m maxed out—let’s pick this up later.”

Notes, Triggers & Red Flags

Known Triggers

  • Music + chemicals together tends to overstimulate.
  • High ambient noise (e.g., crickets at night) can be agitating.
  • Extreme temperatures (hot/humid/cold) drain energy quickly.

Go-To Boundaries

  • Choose quiet over stimulation when unsure.
  • Protect recovery time after social or work intensity.
  • Use short, respectful exits from conversations when flooded.
